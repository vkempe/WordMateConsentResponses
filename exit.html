The WordMate project

This project aims to design a social robot — WordMate — that can interact with children between 2 and 5 years of age in play and book-reading situations, with the aim of facilitating their language development. This involves commenting on their actions, introducing new words, using age-appropriate grammatical complexity, and modelling contingent dialogue. The idea is based on research findings that quantity of language input is predictive of language development. The robot is intended to supplement the language children hear daily. Our project intends to provide proof of concept, but in future such robots could be deployed in community settings like libraries or nurseries to augment children’s linguistic interactions. It is not intended to replace parents and other caregivers.

Community Robotics

Different communities have different ideas about how to talk to children. Because we are designing WordMate in a local context, we want to make sure that it can serve the needs of the local community. This means taking into account how parents and caregivers in Dundee and Scotland feel about supporting their children’s language development: what to talk about and how to do that. Although the present research literature contains some insights into what child-directed speech typically is like, it does not provide sufficient information about the linguistic needs in various communities. We therefore want to involve parents and caregivers in modelling for the robot how it should talk to children, to be authentic and culturally appropriate.

Data handling

We will convert video and audio data into code (e.g., the original video and sound will not be sent to the AI system, but will first be converted into text) that will be used to train an AI system to generate child-directed speech like the one produced by you — the parents and caregivers. To also capture a wider variety of approaches to interaction with children, we will also record our research volunteers talking to children. At no point will any of the personal information be uploaded to any cloud service, which ensures that data about the children could never be accessed by third parties. For full information, please feel free to request our detailed Data Management Plan, which has been approved by Abertay’s Data Protection Officer.

WordMate: Community Robotics for Data Capture to Train a Robot to Facilitate Language Development

Participant information, consent, and demographic form

We invite you to participate in a Community Robotics project where we record caregivers and research volunteers interacting with children to train a robot to interact with children to facilitate their language development. The goal of this phase is to capture video and audio data to train the robot to respond to children in a sensitive, contingent and supportive way. We will record children’s posture, gesture, eye gaze, vocalisations, actions as well as visual context from the caregiver’s perspective and then train an Artificial Intelligence (AI) system to interact with the child like a supportive caregiver would.

We are available for detailed explanations of the project and encourage you to ask questions before deciding to participate. If you do decide to take part, you will be free to withdraw at any time without providing a reason and without penalty.

We also ask you to proceed only if you feel your child is comfortable playing in this situation. You are free to stop the session at any time if you feel your child is uncomfortable.

What will I be required to do?

When you enter the play area in room 3028, you will be asked to wear a head-mounted camera to interact with your child and the surrounding toys in a natural way. We will encourage you to speak to your child in a way that you feel may enhance their language development. You will be asked to do that for how long you feel comfortable, up to 30 minutes maximally. We will start with a warmup period so you can get used to the surroundings.

You will then be asked to sit in the same area as your child while a trained researcher or research volunteer will wear the head-mounted equipment and interact with your child. This person will have been trained to be sensitive to what children like to play with and to use enriching language. This part can also take as much time as the child is comfortable with, up to 30 minutes.

Several of the researchers will also be present to manage the equipment. You or your child can pause or stop the recording at any time if you feel uncomfortable.

At the end of the session, we will invite you to share your thoughts about the project on a brief open-ended questionnaire. Completing this is entirely voluntary — you can still participate in the video recording, even if you do not wish to complete the questionnaire. You may wish to indicate if you are happy for your thoughts on the project to be shared anonymously.

Community Robotics

The success of this project relies on dedication of volunteers like yourself who value the opportunity to contribute to training a robot that interacts with children. As a parent, you are a stakeholder in such a project, and we value your contribution. If you agree, you can be part of a mailing list to receive updates about the project and, if you are interested, invitations to the next phase of interacting with the robot itself. Your email will be kept entirely separately from the research data and will only be used to contact you if you want to stay involved with the project.

How will you handle my data?

Your audio and video data will be converted into fully anonymised code (i.e., the original video and sound will not be sent to the AI system, but the required data will be first converted into text) and used to train an AI system that can provide responses like the ones you have modelled.

We will ask separately for your consent to share short snippets of the video recordings at scientific conferences to demonstrate how this information was used for AI system training. These snippets will be no longer than 1 minute and will only contain your child and your speech but no visuals from yourself. You are absolutely free to withhold this consent and still participate in the project.

We will also ask separately for your consent to share some or all of your statements in the questionnaire anonymously at scientific conferences and in community outreach events to demonstrate how caregivers were involved in co-design and what opinions were expressed. You are absolutely free to withhold this consent and still participate in the project.

Both video data and anonymised code will be kept separately from your email provided for our WordMate Community Robotics mailing list.

The actual recordings will be kept on password-protected University servers for the duration of two years. Only members of the research team will have access to these data. They will be exclusively used for model training.

Abertay University acts as Data Controller (DataProtectionOfficer@abertay.ac.uk).

Retention of research data

Researchers are obliged to retain research data for up to 10 years post-publication; however your anonymised research data may be retained indefinitely (e.g., so that researchers engage in open research, and other researchers can access their data to confirm the conclusions of published work). Consistent with our policy, researchers retain consent forms for as long as we continue to hold information about a data subject and for 10 years for published research (including Research Degree thesis).
